The fuel cut system is particularly effective at higher speeds because it takes maybe 10 seconds to roll to a lower rpm, or more. But... there's something that can throw it off in a bad way. Modern engines have very specific throttle stop screws, and the Idle Detection setting inside the TPS is set precisely to activate when the throttle is miniscule amount's away from fully close in the throttle body. If someone ever removes the TPS from the throttle body, for whatever reason, or if somebody resets the the throttle stop screw height, that can play havoc with your idle detection within your ECU.
